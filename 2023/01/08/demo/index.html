1. Aim

  • Study how to plan to test the parameters of an iron-cored coil.

  • Know how to measure three-phase supply and three-phase circuits.

  • Learn to use Wattmeter , Ammeter and digital multimeter properly.

  • Study measuring the power of single-phase and three-phase circuits.

  • Verify the way to improve power efficiency.

  • Understand the relationship between phase sequence, voltages, rotating directions and speed of AC asynchronous motors.

2. Principle and Applications

2.1 Plan to test the parameters of an iron-cored coil.

  • Test the resistance $$R_L$$ and inductance $$L$$ of the iron-cored coil under line-frequency source by instruments supplied.

  • By connecting 4 different capacitors with the inductor, study the effects on circuit parameters, and find out the capacitance which is needed to be paralleled with the inductor to achieve maximum power factor $$cosФ$$.

2.2 Three-phase circuit

  • Measure the parameters of Y-type loads.

  • Measure the parameters of △-type loads.

2.3 Analysis and discussion for testing parameters of an iron-cored coil.

  • For iron-cored coil, the medium is magnetic material.

  • The resistance $$R_L$$ and inductance $$L$$ will be changing under different AC voltage.

  • $$R_L$$ and L are none-linear with $$U_i$$

  • Measuring and calculating parameters.

    $$P=UIcosФ$$

    $$Z=U/I$$

    $$P=I^2R_L$$

    $$L=\sqrt{Z^2-{R_L}^2/2\pi f}$$

2.4 Three-phase asynchronous motor

  • Consisting of stator and rotator.
  • Change electric energy to mechanical energy.
  • Here, act as 3-phase loads.

5. Tasks

5.1 Iron-cored coil parameters measurement.

  • Based on design plan, measure the parameters in data table. Calculate the values of $$R_L$$ and $$L $$ under given current, and calculate cosφ of inductor.

  • Study the effects on circuit parameters after the inductor is parallel with capacitors. Find the capacitance in order to achieve maximum $$cosφ$$.

  • Observe whether there is any effect when $$I_L$$ changes, to further understand the meaning of component working parameter.

    Notes：

    a. Turn voltage regulator hand shank anticlockwise to the end before switch on the supply.

    b. Keep $$I_L=0.35A$$ while changing capacitor.

    c. The * stands for the capacitance corresponding to the maximum $$cosФ$$.

5.2 Three phase circuit

5.2.1 Y-type loads circuit
  • Construct Y-type loads circuit, and measure the parameters according to the table. The load $$R=510Ω/100W$$.
5.2.2 △-type loads circuit
  • Construct △ -type loads circuit, and measure the parameters according to the table. The load $$R=510Ω/100W$$.

5.3 Observe the rotation of 3-phase asynchronous motor under different connections.

  • Can you propose more kinds of connection? If yes, please practice and observe.
  • Reduce the voltage properly. Is there any change for motor speed?

6. Cautions

  • High voltage, safety in mind anytime!

  • Switch off supply when connecting and disconnecting wires.

  • Any testing meters can not be connected in circuit when switching on or off the source.

  • Use wattmeter properly.

  • Changing meter range with power is prohibited.

  • Testing pen must be taken out of circuits to avoid touching electricity carelessly.

  • Same color wires are better used in each branch in order for inspecting.

$$

$$

​ When the capacitor changing and keep the inductor current $$0.35A$$ , from table 2 we can see that while the capacitor is $$10\mu F$$, the $$cos\phi$$ reaches its maximum value as $$0.99716$$. At that time, we can have :
$$
arccos\phi_{max}=4.32°
$$
If we set the voltage is $$97.31∠0°$$, which means:
$$
I_T=U/Z=0.141∠-4.32°
$$
So, we can draw the picture:

​ We can see that if the capacitor we use is properly, we can reduce the impedance angle to increase the effective power. It can also be use to reduce the current when having the same effective power.

7.1.4 Error analysis

​ While the $$cos\phi$$ reaches its maximum value 1 in the ideal case , we have:
$$
Im(Z)=Im(\frac{R+j\omega L}{1+j\omega RC-\omega^2LC})=0
$$

$$
\Rightarrow C=10.56\mu F
$$

​ So we can see the error in this is mainly because we can’t have the precise capacitance. However, the error is also small.

​ In reality, the main reason of the difference of the powers measured by wattmeter with two methods is that the wattmeter has internal resistance. But under simulation conditions, the difference is mainly because of the changing values on the screen.

7.3 Observe the rotation of 3-phase asynchronous motor under different connections.

7.3.1 Circuit, Data and Explanation
Diagram 9. Two three-phase machine with different wiring methods
Diagram 10. Two three-phase machine with same wiring methods
Diagram 11. Different wiring methods
Diagram 12. Same wiring methods
Diagram 13. Different wiring methods with different input voltage
Diagram 14. Different wiring methods with different input frequency

Explanation:

​ V1 and V2 provided three phase electricity. M1 and M2 are the three-phase machine we used in the circuit. XSC1 can measure the waveform of the core which can reflect the direction of rotation of the rotor.

7.3.2 Analysis:

​ Diagram 11 is the waveform we got when the wiring methods are different, the waveforms are in the different direction which means the direction of rotation of the rotors are different.

​ Diagram 12 is the waveform we got when the wiring methods are same, the waveforms are in the same direction which means the direction of rotation of the rotors are the same.

​ Diagram 13 is the waveform we got when the wiring methods are different and the input voltage of V1 is half of V2. We can see although the amplitudes of the waveforms are different, their frequencies remain the same. So we can say that in the ideal case, the motor speed is independent of the voltage. And in diagram 14 we can see when the input frequency of V1 is half of V2 , we can see the frequency of the waveforms are different, which means the frequency of the rotors are different.

​ What’s more, I search the formula of speed on the Internet:
$$
n = (1−s)n_1= (1−s)\frac{60f_1}{p}
$$
​ In the formula $$n$$ is rotate speed, $$p$$ is number of pole-pairs, $$f_1$$ is the frequency of input and $$s$$ is motor slip. And the formula confirms our conclusion above.

8. Experience

  • In first experiment, I know that when we change the circuit and make the load becomes resistive, we can have a lower current when having the same effective power.

  • In the experiments, I find that we can measure different quantities separately so that the circuit would not be particularly complicated. And it can help us control the circuit and debug.

  • Because this time we use high voltage electricity, so we should be more careful. If we need to change the circuit, we can’t forget to turn off the power first before we operate.

  • From the second experiment we can see neutral-line can prevent loads from interfering with each other.

  • From the last experiment, I find the three-phase asynchronous motor’s direction of rotation depends on wiring methods; and its rotate speed depends on input power frequency.

  • When building a circuit, different branches can use different line colors that it will be easier to be inspected.
